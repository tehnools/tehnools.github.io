    I was born the youngest child in a Christian Family, Singapore. At first I was Conservative Christian, Communist then now I am back to being a Conservative Libetarian.

    Reason for my upbringing as conservative is because of my life in Singapore. It's a very diverse country but at the same time had a good amount of nationalistic pride.

    Singaporeans don't look at each other with race in mind but with merit. It taught me in order to make it in the world you have to be sucessful in order to survive. I learned at my time in New Zealand that that holds true most of the time but some people do need a chance.

    I have never had a time where my values questioned until I entered my national service in Singapore. National Service is conscription where at the age of 18 every Male has to serve for their country. I serve my time for Singapore in the Police Force.

    During a patrol there was a gathering of Indians in the morning under a pavalion in Jalan Bukit Merah, a housing development area in Singapore. There wasn't anything really suspicious to me and my partner wanted to screen them. The Indians got anoyed and wanted to leave. I screened two but one decided to walk away. I felt the whole time that we were just screening them because my partner wanted to fill a quota. So I let that man go. My partner was livid and angry that I let that man go. The security of the people should be put first and I failed that time to fulfil my duty. Yes I may feel that there may be some kind of discrimination because they were foreign nationals but I should never put my nations security over that feeling.

    The consequence of that action affected my relationship with my patrol partner and she didn't have much trust in my abilities at that time which in turn affected my performance in doing my duties as well.

    Strengths and Limitations
