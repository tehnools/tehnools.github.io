Learning Plan

1, May 2019

What is your long term goal and or career pathway?

My long term goal is to be a full stack web developer but still be an og. Another career path I could go to is in networking or game development

What skills (non-technical - human skills) would you like to see developed in yourself while at EDA?

I would like to improve in my communication and time management.

My strengths and limitations

My strengths is that I am reliable, always eager to learn and truthful.

My limitations is that sometimes I can be a little too blunt and I need to be more tactful as well as compassionate to other people. I also need to gain more confidence so I don’t have moments of imposter syndrome.

How I will I manage my workload?

I will try to work on time as well as work together with my teammates. If someone is behind I will help but within reason so eg. When I have completed my work quickly or schedule a time to help. I will try to ask for help form facilitators when I am in need because if I get stuck it will cost too much time and I won’t be on schedule.

How will I committe to seeking help?

I will organize a time with the facilitator or other teammates if I am stuck. I can use slack for quick easy questions.

What do I expect from Dev-Acad facilitators?

I expect that they would be able to help guide me if I need help as well as feedback if I think I have not done my task well.

Will I be busy and block-out some time slots?

Any scheduling information such as block-out times when you are committed to other things. Yes there will be quite a few block out times during my first weeks in Auckland as I need to settle a flat as well as possible job interviews in Auckland.

I always use google calendar to schedule my events/tasks. I’ll try to use toggl to time my workflow.
